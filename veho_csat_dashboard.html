Key Finding: Overall CSAT of 4.836 falls below the 4.9 target. The distribution is heavily skewed toward 5 stars (93.4%), meaning the gap is driven by a relatively small tail of poor experiences — specifically 1-star ratings (2.3%) which act as severe outliers pulling the mean down. Addressing the bottom ~4% of deliveries is the highest-leverage path to reaching 4.9.

CSAT by Delivery Hour (UTC)

Late-night deliveries (10PM+) show dramatic CSAT decline — a clear operational risk zone
Critical Time Finding: Deliveries completed after 10 PM UTC show CSAT dropping sharply — from ~4.87 during peak hours (1-4 PM) to 4.47 at midnight, 4.02 at 1 AM, and as low as 2.15 at 4 AM. Late deliveries are a top driver of poor ratings and likely reflect route overloads, driver fatigue, or capacity planning issues.

Operational Deep-Dive

Delivery duration, instructions adherence, misdelivery impact, and customer voice.

Delivery Duration vs. CSAT

Longer routes correlate with lower satisfaction

Instructions Impact

Packages with delivery instructions get lower CSAT
Instructions Paradox: Packages WITH delivery instructions average 4.751 CSAT vs. 4.885 for those without — a 0.134 gap. This doesn't mean instructions cause bad ratings. Instead, customers who leave instructions have higher expectations and more complex delivery situations (apartments, gate codes, specific placement). Failure to follow these instructions triggers disproportionate dissatisfaction. This is the #1 addressable driver of low CSAT.

Root Cause Themes from Comments:
1. Instructions not followed — placement, gate codes, specific doors
2. Wrong address / misdelivery — even when last_event says "delivered"
3. Late delivery — perishable goods (meal kits) particularly sensitive to timing
4. Driver behavior — rudeness, lack of notification
5. Package condition — damaged, warm food, leaking

Qualitative Analysis & Recommendations

Strategic framework for diagnosing and improving CSAT across Veho's operations.

Why CSAT Matters to Veho

  • Veho's value prop is delivery as "brand extension" — CSAT is the direct measure of that promise
  • Brand clients (Sephora, Zara, Hello Fresh) select logistics partners based on customer experience metrics
  • Low CSAT = contract risk, churn, and competitive vulnerability vs. traditional carriers
  • Driver-partner marketplace quality: CSAT signals help match supply quality to brand demand
  • Perishable / high-value goods amplify consequences of bad delivery experiences

Questions About Current CSAT Methodology

  • What is the CSAT response rate? Are non-respondents randomly distributed or biased?
  • Is the 4.9 target a mean, median, or % at 5-stars?
  • When is the CSAT survey sent? Timing relative to delivery affects response
  • Does CSAT capture delivery experience vs. product experience (e.g. "food was cold")?
  • Are there incentives or prompts that bias toward 5-star ratings?
  • How is CSAT aggregated — by package, route, consumer, or market?

Root Cause Analysis Framework

  • Segment by market × week to identify localized dips vs. systemic trends
  • Correlate low CSAT with: delivery hour, route size, driver tenure, client type
  • Analyze misdelivery → CSAT pipeline (misdelivery = 1.18 avg CSAT)
  • NLP/keyword extraction on low-CSAT comments for thematic clustering
  • Instructions compliance rate — cross-reference instructions text with delivery photos
  • Compare driver CSAT variance to identify coaching vs. systemic issues

Additional Data Requests

  • CSAT response rate per package (to assess selection bias)
  • Driver tenure / experience level (new vs. veteran driver performance)
  • Delivery photos + geolocation accuracy data
  • Client-level SLAs and delivery windows
  • Weather data overlay (impacts perishable deliveries)
  • Route optimization / sequencing data (does order-in-route affect CSAT?)
  • Repeat consumer behavior (does CSAT predict retention?)

Hypotheses for Systematic Improvement

  • Operational: Cap max route completion time; flag routes at risk of late delivery
  • Operational: Enforce instructions compliance via delivery photo + GPS verification
  • Product: Build real-time driver alerts for special instructions (apartment access, placement)
  • Product: Improve consumer ETA accuracy to reduce "late" perception
  • Marketplace: Driver scoring model — route low-risk deliveries to new drivers
  • Marketplace: Performance-based incentives tied to CSAT thresholds
  • Process: Weekly CSAT review cadence per market with ops leads

Cross-Functional Partners

  • Live Operations: Real-time route monitoring, escalation protocols
  • Marketplace / Supply: Driver quality programs, performance incentives
  • Product / Engineering: Driver app UX for instructions, photo verification
  • Data Science: Predictive models for at-risk deliveries
  • Client Success: Align CSAT goals with brand-specific SLAs
  • Expansion: Apply CSAT learnings to new market launches

Recommended Course of Action — Priority Matrix

  • 🔴 Immediate (this week): Identify and coach/pause bottom 8 drivers below 4.0 CSAT. Review late-night delivery policies — consider hard cutoff times.
  • 🟡 Short-term (2-4 weeks): Build automated CSAT alert system per market. Implement instructions-compliance audit on low-CSAT routes. Launch driver-level CSAT dashboard.
  • 🟢 Medium-term (1-3 months): Develop predictive CSAT model (features: hour, route size, driver history, instructions presence). A/B test driver incentive structures tied to CSAT. Redesign CSAT survey to separate delivery experience from product experience.
  • 🔵 Strategic (3-6 months): Build instructions-compliance scoring via NLP + photo analysis. Integrate CSAT into driver marketplace ranking algorithm. Establish CSAT as a north-star metric with cross-functional OKRs.
